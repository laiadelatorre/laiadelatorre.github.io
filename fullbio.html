About Laia

Laia is a classical pianist from a town near Barcelona. Her passion as a solo pianist goes hand in hand with her deep interest in the world of composition and improvisation.

She got graduated at Conservatori Superior de Música del Liceu of Barcelona under the teaching of Àlex Alguacil in piano and chamber music with Emili Brugalla. Throughout her pianistic career, she has had the opportunity to play for world-renowned pianists in masterclasses, such as Ya-Fei Chuang, Ian Jones, Danny Driver, Péter Nagy, Vasco Dantas, Congyu Wang, Stephanie Baer, Akiko Ebi, Ilya Maximov and Yejin Gil among others. At the same time she was studying her music undergraduate studies, she also studied the Bachelor in Biology at the University of Barcelona, followed by a Master in Genetics and Genomics in the same university.

Her soloist career has always been accompanied with her devotion to chamber music. With Trio Aqua, founded in 2018, she has performed in international stages and has participated in masterclasses by great musicians, such as Adrian Brendel, Péter Nagy, Salvador Brotons, Luc-Marie Aguera and Jan Bjøranger. She also collaborates regularly with the violinist Jardiel Rodellas, playing in fusion music concerts, where they combine different musical styles. Previously to these events, she obtained the 3rd prize in IX Concurs de Música de Cambra Higini Anglès (2013).

All together, it has led Laia to perform in many emblematic stages, both as a soloist and as a chamber pianist: the Teatro das Figuras (Faro), Auditori del Tívoli (el Vendrell), Jardí dels Tarongers (Barcelona), Sala Parés (Barcelona), Reial Cercle Artístic de Barcelona, Nathan Milstein Hall (Estocolm), Kleines Studio (Salzburg) and at the Real Collegio (Lucca) among others. This summer she debuted with the Orquestra do Algarve with Chopin's Piano Concerto in E minor, under the baton of Joshua dos Santos.

Since a very young age, Laia has been interested in composing music and some of her pieces has been awarded and premiered. In 2023, her composition "Like Water" won the 2nd Prize at Leonardo4Music Competition, which afterwards was premiered at BOZAR in Brussels. Also, her arrangement of Sweet festivities (Les Dolces Festes) was selected by the ECN Christmas Competition and premiered in Lisbon. Nowadays, she is being tutored by the composer Mike Ladouceur. Finally, Laia has been commissioned to compose the music for a new ballet play this year. 

On the other hand, she has also delved into the world of musical improvisation, learning this craft from renowned pianists such as Carles Marigó, Emilio Molina and Juan Maria Cisneros, and the application of this field into dance accompaniment through Kim Helweg, Mariana Palacios, Alberto de Paz and Juanjo Ochoa. She currently plays for professional dancers in the High-Performance Centre of Dance in Terrassa (PAR) and in IEA Oriol Martorell, accompanying classical, contemporary and spanish dancers and playing for internationally-known maestros, as Rodolfo Castellanos, Anael Martín, Ilya Jivoy, William Castro, Magalí Suárez and Jia Yong Sun among others. 

She likes to share her interests in all these fields on her YouTube channel.
